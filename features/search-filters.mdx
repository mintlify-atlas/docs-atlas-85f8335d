Popcorn Vision offers powerful search and filtering tools to help you discover content tailored to your preferences.

## Quick Search

The fastest way to find specific movies or TV shows:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Search">
    Click the search icon in the navigation bar or press `/` on your keyboard
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Your Query">
    Type the title of a movie, TV show, or person's name
  </Step>
  <Step title="View Results">
    Results appear instantly as you type, showing matching titles with posters and key information
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  When using quick search (query mode), advanced filters are automatically disabled. Clear your search query to use filters.
</Warning>

## Advanced Search Page

Access the advanced search page for comprehensive filtering:

- **For Movies**: Navigate to `/search`
- **For TV Shows**: Navigate to `/tv/search`

## Available Filters

### Release Date

Filter content by release date or air date range:

<Info>
  The release date filter shows the oldest and newest available titles in the database, covering decades of content.
</Info>

**How to use:**
1. Click the start date picker to select your minimum release date
2. Click the end date picker to select your maximum release date
3. Results update automatically to show only titles within your date range

**Example use cases:**
- Find classic movies from the 1980s
- Discover what's coming out next month
- Browse shows that aired in a specific year

### Streaming Providers

Find content available on your streaming services:

<Tip>
  The streaming filter automatically detects your location and shows providers available in your region.
</Tip>

**Supported operators:**
- **AND**: Shows content available on ALL selected platforms
- **OR**: Shows content available on ANY of the selected platforms

**Popular providers include:**
- Netflix
- Amazon Prime Video
- Disney+
- Hulu
- HBO Max
- Apple TV+
- Paramount+
- And many more based on your region

### Genre

Browse by genre with flexible combinations:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="AND Operator">
    **Use when**: You want content that matches ALL selected genres
    
    **Example**: Action AND Comedy = Action comedies only
    
    Results are more specific and focused.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="OR Operator">
    **Use when**: You want content from ANY of the selected genres
    
    **Example**: Horror OR Thriller = All horror movies plus all thrillers
    
    Results are broader and more diverse.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Available genres** include Action, Adventure, Animation, Comedy, Crime, Documentary, Drama, Family, Fantasy, Horror, Mystery, Romance, Sci-Fi, Thriller, and more.

### Cast & Crew (Movies Only)

Find movies featuring specific actors or directors:

#### Actor Filter

1. Click in the Actor field
2. Start typing an actor's name
3. Select from the autocomplete suggestions
4. Add multiple actors using AND/OR operators

**Examples:**
- **AND**: Movies featuring Leonardo DiCaprio AND Tom Hanks together
- **OR**: Movies with Leonardo DiCaprio OR Tom Hanks

#### Crew Filter

Similar to the actor filter but for directors, writers, and other crew:

1. Click in the Crew field
2. Type a director or crew member's name
3. Select from suggestions
4. Use AND/OR for multiple selections

<Tip>
  The search uses intelligent autocomplete that queries TMDB's person database as you type.
</Tip>

### Production Company

Filter by the studio or production company:

1. Start typing a company name (e.g., "Warner Bros", "Marvel Studios")
2. Select from autocomplete suggestions
3. Use AND/OR operators for multiple companies

**Use cases:**
- Find all Marvel Cinematic Universe films
- Browse Pixar's complete catalog
- Discover A24's indie releases

### Language

Filter content by original language:

- English
- Spanish
- French
- Japanese
- Korean
- German
- Italian
- And many more languages

<Note>
  The language filter refers to the original language of the content, not dubbed or subtitle options.
</Note>

### Keywords

Search by keywords and themes:

1. Type a keyword (e.g., "based on novel", "superhero", "time travel")
2. Select from autocomplete suggestions
3. Combine multiple keywords with AND/OR operators

**Popular keywords:**
- Based on novel
- Superhero
- Time travel
- Coming of age
- Post-apocalyptic
- Artificial intelligence

### Runtime

Filter by movie or episode length (in minutes):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Adjust Minimum">
    Drag the left slider handle to set minimum runtime (0-300 minutes)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust Maximum">
    Drag the right slider handle to set maximum runtime
  </Step>
  <Step title="View Results">
    Results automatically update to show content within your runtime range
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Common ranges:**
- Short films: 0-60 minutes
- Standard movies: 90-120 minutes
- Long films: 150+ minutes

### Rating

Filter by average user rating (0-10 scale):

- Use the dual slider to set minimum and maximum ratings
- Based on TMDB community ratings
- Step increment: 0.1

**Examples:**
- 8.0-10.0: Highly acclaimed films
- 7.0-8.0: Well-received content
- 5.0-7.0: Mixed reviews

<Info>
  Ratings are based on TMDB's community voting system and are updated regularly.
</Info>

### Rating Count

Filter by the number of ratings received:

- Useful for finding popular content vs. hidden gems
- Higher vote counts generally indicate more mainstream content
- Lower counts may reveal underrated or niche titles

### TV Series Status (TV Shows Only)

Filter shows by production status:

- **Returning Series**: Currently airing with more seasons planned
- **Ended**: Series has concluded
- **Canceled**: Series was canceled before planned completion
- **In Production**: Currently filming new episodes
- **Planned**: Announced but not yet in production

### TV Series Type (TV Shows Only)

Filter by the type of TV series:

- **Scripted**: Traditional scripted series
- **Reality**: Reality TV shows
- **Documentary**: Documentary series
- **News**: News programs
- **Talk Show**: Talk shows and interviews
- **Miniseries**: Limited series with predetermined episode count

## Sorting Results

Sort your filtered results by:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Popularity">
    Shows most popular content first (default)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Release Date">
    Sort by newest or oldest
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Rating">
    Sort by highest or lowest rated
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Title">
    Alphabetical sorting A-Z or Z-A
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Vote Count">
    Sort by number of user ratings
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Sort order:**
- **Descending**: Highest to lowest (default)
- **Ascending**: Lowest to highest

## Combining Multiple Filters

<Warning>
  Filters work together to narrow your results. The more filters you apply, the more specific your results become.
</Warning>

### Example Searches

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Find Sci-Fi Movies on Netflix">
    1. Go to `/search`
    2. Set **Genre**: Science Fiction
    3. Set **Streaming**: Netflix
    4. Optionally add **Release Date** for recent titles
    5. Sort by **Rating** to see the best first
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Discover Upcoming Action Movies">
    1. Go to `/search`
    2. Set **Genre**: Action
    3. Set **Release Date**: Today to 3 months from now
    4. Sort by **Release Date** (Ascending) to see what's coming soonest
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Browse Classic Horror Films">
    1. Go to `/search`
    2. Set **Genre**: Horror
    3. Set **Release Date**: 1970-1990
    4. Set **Rating**: 7.0-10.0
    5. Sort by **Rating** (Descending)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Find Shows with Your Favorite Actor">
    1. Go to `/tv/search`
    2. Set **Cast**: Type and select actor name
    3. Set **Status**: Returning Series (for active shows)
    4. Sort by **Popularity**
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Filter Sidebar

On desktop, the filter sidebar appears on the left side of the search page:

- **Toggle visibility**: Click the filter icon to show/hide
- **Sticky positioning**: Sidebar scrolls with you for easy access
- **Mobile view**: Full-screen overlay with close button

<Tip>
  Applied filters appear as chips in the search results area. Click any chip to quickly remove that filter.
</Tip>

## Tips for Better Searching

1. **Start broad, then narrow**: Begin with one or two filters and add more to refine
2. **Use OR operators**: Great for exploring multiple options at once
3. **Adjust date ranges**: Release date is powerful for finding new or classic content
4. **Check streaming availability**: Set your location for accurate provider information
5. **Combine keywords**: Keywords + genre can find very specific content themes
